# Venue and Brand Guide

Use this guide before choosing layouts.

## Venue Profile

Define a venue profile that is specific enough to constrain design decisions.

Recommended fields:

- name
- concept
- cuisine or offer type
- service model
- price range
- audience
- location context
- tone

Practical rule: if the venue name and concept could be swapped into ten unrelated menu boards without anyone noticing, the profile is too vague.

## Venue Questions

Ask or infer:

1. Is the venue quick-service, counter-service, table-service, or mixed?
2. Is the board intended to drive speed, appetite, premium perception, or service clarity?
3. Is the menu stable, seasonal, or mode-based through the day?
4. Is the venue branded as playful, premium, artisan, healthy, bold, or minimal?

## Brand Profile

Define a usable brand system before layout work.

Minimum fields:

- logo direction
- colour palette
- typography pairing
- price styling direction
- icon style
- image style
- background or surface style

## Brand Rules

- Use one clear accent colour if it improves hierarchy.
- Make price styling belong to the same visual system as headings.
- Keep imagery, typography, and iconography in one recognisable tone.
- Avoid polished premium typography paired with cheap-looking stock imagery.

## Example Venue and Brand Pair

### Venue

- name: Forge & Fern
- concept: urban grill with lunch bowls and evening flatbreads
- service model: mixed counter and table collection
- tone: bold, warm, modern

### Brand

- background: charcoal
- foreground: warm stone
- accent: ember orange
- headings: condensed industrial sans
- body and prices: clean neo-grotesk
- image style: dramatic side-lit food photography with dark surfaces

The venue and brand should already imply a different menu-board result than a bakery, juice bar, or concessions stand.
